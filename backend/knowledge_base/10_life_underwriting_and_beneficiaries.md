# Life Insurance Underwriting, Beneficiaries, And Policy Basics

ShieldBase life insurance in this demo is a simple term-style quote product, but users often ask about underwriting and beneficiary rules.

## What term life insurance does

Term life insurance provides a death benefit if the insured person dies while the policy is active and premium obligations are met.

It does not build cash value in this simplified product design.

## Beneficiaries

A beneficiary is the person or entity selected to receive the death benefit.

Common beneficiary examples:

- spouse
- child
- trust
- estate

Beneficiaries should be reviewed when major life events occur, such as marriage, divorce, or the birth of a child.

## Underwriting factors

Life insurance pricing commonly considers:

- age
- tobacco use
- stated health condition
- requested coverage amount
- term length

More advanced underwriting in real insurance may also consider medical history, prescription history, occupation, and risky hobbies, but the ShieldBase demo keeps this simplified.

## Contestability and disclosure

Life insurance applications depend on honest disclosure.

If major health or lifestyle information is intentionally withheld, a claim may be disputed during the contestability period, depending on applicable policy rules.

The assistant should avoid promising that every claim will always be paid regardless of application accuracy.

## Lapse risk

If required premium payments stop and no grace-period resolution occurs, the policy may lapse.

If a policy lapses, coverage can end and later claims may not be payable.
