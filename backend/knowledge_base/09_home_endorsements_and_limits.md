# Home Coverage Limits, Exclusions, And Optional Add-Ons

ShieldBase home insurance in this demo focuses on core property protection, but users often need clarification on limits and exclusions.

## Core home coverage areas

Typical home coverage includes:

- dwelling coverage for the structure
- personal property coverage for belongings
- liability coverage for certain injury or damage claims
- loss of use or additional living expenses after a covered loss

## Common exclusions

Standard home coverage usually does not include:

- flood damage
- earthquake damage
- sewer backup unless added separately
- gradual leaks or unresolved maintenance issues
- mold caused by neglected maintenance
- normal wear and tear

These exclusions matter because many users assume "home insurance covers all water damage," which is usually incorrect.

## Policy limits

Home insurance is subject to limits, even when a loss is covered.

Examples:

- personal property may have sub-limits for jewelry, collectibles, or electronics
- detached structures may have separate limits
- temporary housing reimbursement may be capped by policy terms

## Optional add-ons or endorsements

Depending on product design, customers may ask about optional protections such as:

- scheduled personal property coverage
- sewer or drain backup protection
- higher liability limits
- equipment breakdown style coverage
- identity theft assistance

The assistant should explain that these are examples of optional enhancements, not guarantees in every quote.

## Deductible expectations

Like auto insurance, home policies often include a deductible.

The deductible is the amount the customer pays before the insurer contributes to a covered property loss.

Higher deductibles usually lower premium estimates.

## Common user questions this document should support

- Does home insurance cover floods?
- Does home insurance cover earthquakes?
- What is covered under personal property?
- Are expensive items fully covered?
- What is a home insurance deductible?
