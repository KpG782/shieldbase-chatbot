# Common Claims Scenarios And Coverage Examples

Users often ask for concrete examples instead of abstract definitions. This document gives scenario-style explanations across auto, home, and life insurance.

## Auto examples

### Collision example

If a driver hits another car or a guardrail, that is usually a collision event.

Collision coverage is commonly associated with damage to the insured vehicle after a crash, subject to deductibles and policy terms.

### Comprehensive example

If a parked car is stolen or damaged by hail, that is commonly treated as a comprehensive non-collision event.

### Liability example

If the insured driver causes damage to another person's vehicle or injuries to another person, liability coverage is the part most users should think about first.

## Home examples

### Covered fire example

If a kitchen fire damages the home structure, the dwelling portion of the policy may respond, subject to the deductible and policy limits.

### Non-covered flood example

If rising floodwater enters the home after a storm, that is commonly excluded from a standard home policy unless separate flood protection exists.

### Temporary living expense example

If a covered loss makes the home uninhabitable, additional living expense coverage may help with temporary housing costs, up to policy limits.

## Life examples

### Active term policy example

If the insured dies while the term policy is active and the policy remains in force, beneficiaries may receive the selected death benefit under policy terms.

### Lapsed policy example

If the policy is no longer active because required premium obligations were not maintained, coverage may not apply.

## Assistant behavior guidance

When users ask scenario questions, the assistant should:

- explain the likely coverage category
- mention exclusions or limits when relevant
- avoid guaranteeing claim approval
- state when additional review would be needed

## Common user questions this document should support

- Would hail damage be collision or comprehensive?
- Does home insurance cover fire?
- Does home insurance cover flood damage?
- What happens if my life policy lapses?
- Which part of auto insurance covers damage I cause to someone else?
