# Auto Coverage Levels And Deductibles

ShieldBase auto insurance uses three simplified coverage levels for quoting: basic, standard, and comprehensive.

## Basic

Basic coverage is designed for customers who want lower premium estimates and essential protection.

Typical elements:

- liability coverage
- state-minimum style protection assumptions
- limited optional add-ons

This level is usually best for:

- older vehicles
- drivers prioritizing lower monthly cost
- customers comfortable taking on more out-of-pocket risk

## Standard

Standard coverage is meant to balance price and protection.

Typical elements:

- higher liability limits than basic
- collision coverage
- comprehensive coverage
- optional rental or roadside features depending on the quote

This level is usually best for:

- daily drivers
- financed vehicles
- customers who want broader protection without choosing the highest tier

## Comprehensive

Comprehensive is the highest quote tier in the ShieldBase demo flow.

Typical elements:

- broader liability assumptions
- collision coverage
- comprehensive non-collision protection
- stronger optional coverage assumptions

This level is usually best for:

- newer vehicles
- customers who want stronger financial protection
- users asking for broader coverage against theft, weather, or vandalism

## What comprehensive means in auto insurance

Comprehensive coverage refers to non-collision damage to the insured vehicle.

Common examples:

- theft
- vandalism
- falling objects
- hail or storm damage
- fire
- animal impact in some policy structures

It does not mean "covers everything." It still has exclusions, limits, and usually a deductible.

## What a deductible means

A deductible is the amount the policyholder pays before covered physical damage benefits apply.

Example:

- vehicle repair cost: $2,500
- deductible: $500
- insurer-covered amount: $2,000

Higher deductibles usually reduce premium.
Lower deductibles usually increase premium.
